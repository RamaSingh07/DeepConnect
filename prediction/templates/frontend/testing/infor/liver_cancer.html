Liver Cancer: Understanding, Preventing, and Managing

Explore the causes, symptoms, treatment options, and prevention of liver cancer. Learn how early detection can save lives.

Overview of Liver Cancer

Liver cancer occurs when abnormal cells grow uncontrollably in the liver. It can be primary (starting in the liver) or secondary (spreading from another part of the body).Liver cancer happens when liver cells develop changes (mutations) in their DNA.

Read More

Causes & Risk Factors

Hepatitis infections, obesity, and genetic factors contribute to liver cancer risks.People with HCV-related cirrhosis have a higher risk of developing liver cancer than people with cirrhosis related to HBV , heavy alcohol consumption are major risk factors for liver disease.

Learn More

Symptoms & Diagnosis

Symptoms include jaundice, abdominal pain, and unexplained weight loss. Imaging tests and biopsies help confirm the diagnosis.In most cases, doctors use a CT scan to diagnose liver cancer.Blood tests used to assess the liver are known as liver function tests.

Explore More

Treatment Options

From surgery to targeted therapies, treatment depends on the stage of the cancer and overall health.Liver transplantation has proven to be the most effective treatment for patients with hepatocellular carcinoma, a common type of liver cancer.
