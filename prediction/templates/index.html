Liver Health Parameters

Discover key indicators that help predict and monitor liver cirrhosis. Explore detailed insights into each parameter below.

Age

Age plays a significant role in understanding liver health. It's an important indicator for cirrhosis risk and progression.

The risk of liver issues often increases with age.

Gender

Gender differences can influence liver conditions. Studies suggest variations in liver cirrhosis risk between males and females.

Hormonal differences may impact liver health.

Total Bilirubin

Total bilirubin levels indicate liver function and help detect abnormalities. Elevated levels may suggest liver disease.

Normal levels are essential for healthy liver function.

Direct Bilirubin

Direct bilirubin measures the liver's ability to excrete bile. It's a critical factor in diagnosing liver cirrhosis.

Abnormal levels often suggest liver obstruction.

Alkaline Phosphatase

This enzyme level is essential for understanding liver and bone health. Elevated levels may indicate bile duct issues.

Crucial for diagnosing liver conditions.

SGOT

SGOT levels reflect liver cell damage and help monitor liver conditions like cirrhosis and hepatitis.

High SGOT levels indicate liver stress.

Albumin

Albumin is a protein made by the liver. Its levels provide insights into liver function and overall health.

Low albumin levels may indicate chronic liver disease.

Prothrombin Time

Prothrombin time measures blood clotting ability. Prolonged times could indicate liver issues.

A key test for liver function assessment.
